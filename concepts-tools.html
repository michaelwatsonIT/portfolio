IT Concepts and Tools

Course Overview

Lecturer : Ian Purdon

This course provided a comprehensive introduction to IT in organisational contexts, covering essential concepts in professional conduct, legal and ethical principles, data communication, and business communication. Through this course, I developed both theoretical knowledge and practical skills using industry-standard tools.

Key Topics Covered

1. IT in Organisations

Understanding how information technology is integrated into organisational structures and processes.
Particularily the Five Critical Functions of IT: Planning, Networks, Data Management, IT Support, and Security.

2. Professional Conduct and Social Responsibility

Exploring ethical responsibilities and professional standards in the IT industry.
Covering Professionalism, Sustainable Practice, and Social Responsibility.

3. Legal and Ethical Principles

Examining the legal frameworks and ethical guidelines that govern IT practices.
Such as Copyright, Privacy and Unsolicited Communications.

4. Data Communication

Learning the fundamentals of data transmission, protocols, and communication systems.
Exploration of Microsoft Office tools, and Data interpretation and demonstration.

5. Communication

Developing effective written and verbal communication skills essential for IT professionals.
Looking at Communication styles, soft skills, oral and visual presentation ability and cultural awareness.
